About

Hello, I’m Emily. I am a graphic designer and junior web developer, with a focus on front-end web development and an interest in accesibility. When I am not perfecting my code, I am learning Spanish or dreaming of the Turks and Caicos.

When I had the task of creating a website, I was so excited and I had so many ideas. I knew I wanted it to be feminine, bright, and fun— much like me! I wanted the visitor to immediately know who I am as a person.

Thank you for taking the time to view my site! Enjoy your stay!! 🧿

Here was my design process!

Creating a Moodboard

My process began with creating a moodboard. I settled on a retro beach theme with in-your-face colours. Once I decided on the colours, I began a low-fidelity wireframe along with deciding on what images I wanted on my site.

Making My Logo

Now I needed a logo! I wanted it to be simple and obvious. I thought the blobs were organic yet intentional. I wanted to use my initials, and when I did the lowercase “e” and “c,” it made an uppercase cursive “E” which was perfect. I was happy with my logo so I moved onto the wireframe.

Making a High Fidelity Wireframe

I made all of the art on my website using Adobe Illustrator. Once I had my art and logo, I made my high-fidelity wireframe on Adobe XD. This process wasn't truly over until I was finished coding my art onto the site. This was a fun step because I am a planner, so laying out all of my text and images allowed me to see my website shine through.

The Coding Process: HTML & CSS

Finally, I had to start coding it. This is where I had some fun. I also spent an unspeakable amount of time getting my images in the perfect position. But CSS is where my site finally started coming together. I enjoyed this part of the process because after I did one line of code, I got to refresh the site and see all of my work come to life!
